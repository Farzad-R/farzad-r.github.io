Wind power forecasting (WPF) is a challenging time-series forecasting task due to the non-linear, dynamic nature of wind farms and the chaotic nature of atmospheric wind speeds and directions. Previous studies have often relied on proprietary, facility-specific wind farm data to advance WPF models. However, my PhD research has led me to develop a novel framework and deep learning architecture named WindX (temporary name), which does not require any facility-specific data except the average wind farm's output power. This model outperforms state-of-the-art deep learning time-series forecasting models such as LSTNet and FEDformer, as well as the industry standard forecast model utilized by the Independent Electricity System Operator (IESO). WindX achieves on average 86.18% improvement over competitive models. The result of model interpretation using the Integrated Gradients will be added to this report as well. In addition to superior predictive accuracy, the model is more computationally efficient than competitors and has the potential to drastically improve wind power deployment and achievement of net-zero greenhouse gas emission goals.

Experimental setting

The experiments were conducted using data from two real-world wind farms located in Ontario, Canada. The test set comprised one year of average output power data from each wind farm. The experiments were carried out on a computer running the Linux Ubuntu operating system, equipped with an NVIDIA Corporation TU102GL [Quadro RTX 8000] GPU and 64GB of RAM.
