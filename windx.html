WindX: Short-term and Mid-term Wind Power Prediction using Real-world Data

Author: Farzad Roozitalab

Welcome to my web page showcasing my wind power forecasting model. Please note that the name "WindX" is a temporary placeholder and does not represent the final name of the model. Additionally, I have not included technical details of the model architecture in this report as the framework is currently undergoing the review process for publication and also the process of becoming a patent. As soon as the paperwork process is complete, I will provide further updates and technical details about the model.
The purpose of this report is to showcase the performance of my wind power forecasting model on two active wind farms located in Ontario, Canada. These wind farms serve as benchmarks to evaluate the accuracy and reliability of the model's forecasting capabilities.

Wind power forecasting (WPF) is a challenging time-series forecasting task due to the non-linear, dynamic nature of wind farms and the chaotic nature of atmospheric wind speeds and directions. Previous studies have often relied on proprietary, facility-specific wind farm data to advance WPF models. However, for the first project for my PhD, I developed a new framework and a new deep learning architecture that does not take ane of those inputs except the average wind farm's output power and yet it is capable of capturing complex patterns in the data. WindX significantly outperforms state-of-the-art deep learning time-series forecasting models, like LSTNet and FEDformer, as well as the industry standard forecast model utilized by the Independent Electricity System Operator (IESO). WindX achieves an average 75.8% improvement over competitive models, with an average MSE of 0.0053 on a 12-hour-ahead WPF, and an average 1-hour-ahead MSE of 0.001. The result of model interpretation using the Integrated Gradients will be added to this report as well. In addition to superior predictive accuracy, the model is more computationally efficient than competitors and has the potential to drastically improve wind power deployment and achievement of net-zero greenhouse gas emission goals.

Model performance on wind farms

By considering the uncertainty of the raw data, I have been able to improve the model's ability to accurately forecast wind power generation. This approach provides a more realistic assessment of the model's performance, which can be useful in a range of practical applications.
